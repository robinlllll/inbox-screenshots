# Summary

The podcast explores the performance and tactical application of AppLovin as an advertising platform for e-commerce businesses. It highlights the positive incrementality observed across multiple tests, where AppLovin's internal measurements consistently underestimate the actual revenue impact. The discussion covers best practices for account setup, emphasizing the ease of integration with Shopify due to its in-depth pixel tracking. Speakers also delve into campaign structure, creative strategies, and bidding options, noting the platform's unique feature of guaranteed ad exposure through longer, unskippable video ads within mobile games. The hosts predict a significant increase in investment in AppLovin for the coming year, potentially making it a major player alongside Meta and Google Ads.

# Takeaways

* AppLovin's measurement of business impact is conservative due to its exclusive use of sharp, click-based attribution (one-day or same-day).
* Incrementality tests on AppLovin consistently show positive business impact, with revenue impact exceeding platform-reported impact.
* AppLovin's Shopify integration simplifies account setup, pixel firing, server events, and feed integration, often achievable within 10 minutes.
* A consolidated campaign structure is optimal for new AppLovin accounts during the initial 14-day learning period.
* Utilizing 30+ second video ads on AppLovin is advantageous because the platform guarantees a minimum of 10 seconds watch time.
* AppLovin's algorithm favors ad creative videos with a median exposure of 35 seconds.
* Applying the average incrementality factor (e.g., 151%) to a client's AMER target ROAS can inform bid settings on AppLovin.
* AppLovin's prospecting campaign, which focuses on new customer acquisition, has demonstrated a 3x increase in new customer traffic, as measured by Northbeam.
* AppLovin's share of total media investment for Black Friday and Cyber Monday is predicted to increase from under 5% to 15-20% in 2026.
* The peak investment in AppLovin during Black Friday/Cyber Monday occurred on Monday rather than Friday, indicating its performance and ability to absorb media dollars quickly.

# Q & A

**Q: What makes AppLovin an exciting channel for e-commerce advertisers, and what results have you seen from incrementality tests?**

A: AppLovin stands out as a particularly exciting channel, especially with the introduction of its self-service portal. We've conducted approximately eight completed incrementality tests, and every new project starts with one. The consistent finding is that AppLovin measures as positively incremental. The platform's conservative measurement, using only sharp, click-based attribution (either one-day or same-day), means that the actual business impact is often larger than what the platform reportsâ€”typically showing incrementality percentages between 120% and 150%.

---

**Q: How should advertisers approach creative strategy on AppLovin, given its unique ad format within mobile games?**

A: Given that AppLovin serves ads within mobile games with a guaranteed minimum of 10 seconds watch time, advertisers should prioritize longer, engaging video content. There's no way to click out of the ads, so there's no benefit to not using 30+ second videos. This provides a unique canvas to work with, allowing for more creative storytelling and the opportunity to hook viewers beyond the initial few seconds, unlike scrolly social media where attention spans are shorter.

---

**Q: How does AppLovin's measurement compare to other platforms like Meta and YouTube, and what does this imply for advertiser confidence?**

A: AppLovin's incrementality measurements have shown a tight band of results across tests, similar to Meta's 7-Day Click attribution, which gives us a higher level of confidence. In contrast, YouTube incrementality tests often yield wider bands, making it more challenging to have confidence in the results. The consistent measurement from AppLovin leads to an ever-increasing level of confidence that we can be confident that we're helping create contribution margin with this investment.

---

**Q: What are some best practices for account setup on AppLovin, particularly for Shopify stores?**

A: Setting up an AppLovin account for a Shopify store is remarkably simple. By installing the Shopify app, the pixel and server events are automatically fired, and the product feed is pulled directly from the Shopify store into AppLovin. The entire setup process can take as little as 10 minutes. However, for non-Shopify stores, the setup is more technically involved, requiring more resources from a developer standpoint to trigger all the necessary events.

---

**Q: How should advertisers think about campaign structure on AppLovin, specifically regarding consolidation versus segmentation?**

A: When starting a new account on AppLovin, it's best to begin with one consolidated campaign to ramp up through the learning period and apply the incrementality test. After that, consider segmenting campaigns based on factors like Average Order Value (AOV) to ensure spend is directed towards higher-value products. Since bidding is set at the campaign level, AOV becomes a key trigger for segmentation.

---

**Q: What bidding options are available on AppLovin, and how should advertisers approach them?**

A: AppLovin initially offered only Cost Per Purchase (CPP) bidding but has since added Target ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) bidding and a new customer acquisition bidding option. While both CPP and ROAS bidding are viable, I've seen a little bit more efficiency and scale from Target ROAS. It's also worth exploring the prospecting campaign type with the new customer acquisition bidding, especially for larger clients with significant customer files.

---

**Q: How can advertisers leverage AppLovin's prospecting campaign and new customer acquisition bidding?**

A: We demoed the prospecting function in AppLovin, and we saw like a 3x increase in the North Beam percent new customer traffic. The prospecting campaign, combined with new customer acquisition bidding, can be highly effective in reaching net new eyeballs. This is particularly valuable for brands with large existing customer files, where it's crucial to focus on acquiring new customers rather than retargeting existing ones.

---

**Q: What is the significance of AppLovin's pixel, and how does it contribute to effective advertising?**

A: AppLovin's pixel is quite in-depth, tracking a large number of events, including page view, view content, add to cart, payment info, checkout, and purchase. This richer data set allows the pixel to find the right audience more effectively. All those events help that pixel find the right audience.

My prediction for this coming year, in 2026, is that AppLovin is going to be 15%, 20% of the total investment in Black Friday and Cyber Monday.
